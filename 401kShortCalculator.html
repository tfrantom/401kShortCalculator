▸ model notes — how the simulation works 
KEY INSIGHT: The 401k loan lives inside your account.
Repaying principal = buying shares back at the current market price.
Interest is the only real cost that leaves the system.

BASELINE each month i:
  shares   *= (1 + g/12)          appreciation
  shares   += C / price[i]        contribution buys shares at market
  tax_saved += C * t              pre-tax contribution saves t per dollar

LOAN STRATEGY each month i:
  shares   *= (1 + g/12)          appreciation (on reduced share count)
  shares   += Cl / price[i]       reduced contribution buys shares
  shares   += principalPortion_i / price[i]   payment buys back shares!
  tax_saved += Cl * t

At month k (lump-sum day):
  shares   += Bk / price[k]       lump sum buys remaining shares at bottom price

ΔV_shares = (loanShares - baseShares) * price[k]
            = the share count difference valued at payoff price

Costs subtracted from ΔV_shares (all paid from income, not shares):
  interestGrossUp  = totalInterest * t / (1-t)     interest paid w/ after-tax $
  interestRetireTax = totalInterest * tw / (1+d)^W  taxed again at retirement
  contribTaxDiff   = (C - Cl) * t * k * PV_factor  baseline got more tax savings
  defaultCost      = defProb * Bk_mid * (tw + penalty)

deltaV_final = ΔV_shares * (1-tw)/(1+d)^W - interestGrossUp - interestRetireTax
             - contribTaxDiff - defaultCost
